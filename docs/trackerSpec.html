NHS Wales Hospital Paper Case Note Tracking System – Specification

1. Purpose and Context

This specification defines a standardised paper case note tracking system for hospitals in NHS Wales. It supports safe, efficient management of physical health records while aligning with the NHS Wales Records Management Code of Practice for Health and Social Care 2022 and local information governance arrangements.

The system is intended as a transitional and fallback mechanism alongside ongoing digital transformation (e.g. Welsh Nursing Care Record and electronic health records), ensuring that paper case notes remain locatable, auditable and secure across all hospital sites.

2. Scope

  • All paper case notes for inpatients, outpatients and emergency attendances.
  • All hospital departments within an NHS Wales Health Board or Trust.
  • Movements of case notes between:
    • Medical Records libraries
    • Clinical areas (wards, clinics, theatres)
    • Diagnostic departments
    • Off-site storage providers
  • Tracking of temporary paper records created when the main file is unavailable.

3. Objectives

  • Ensure that the current physical location of any case note is known at all times.
  • Support timely availability of records for patient care and legal/statutory purposes.
  • Provide an auditable trail of movements and custody for each case note.
  • Reduce misfiles, lost notes and duplicate temporary records.
  • Comply with retention, confidentiality and security requirements defined in national codes and local policies.

4. System Overview

The tracking system combines:

  • Unique identifiers on each case note (e.g. NHS number plus local case note number and barcode).
  • Standardised tracking labels and movement forms.
  • A central tracking register (electronic or paper-based) maintained by Medical Records.
  • Departmental tracking logs for local custody and sub-movements.
  • Defined workflows for requesting, issuing, returning and archiving case notes.

5. Data Model

5.1 Core Entities

  • Patient: NHS number, name, date of birth, primary Health Board.
  • Case Note File: local case note number, volume number, barcode, record type (e.g. main, maternity, mental health).
  • Location: coded list of physical locations (e.g. MR01 – Main Records Library, WD23 – Ward 23, CL05 – Outpatient Clinic 5, OS01 – Off-site Store 1).
  • Movement: movement ID, case note file, from-location, to-location, date/time, requester, courier/porter, receiver, purpose (clinic, investigation, audit, legal, etc.).
  • Custodian: person or team currently responsible for the case note at a location.

5.2 Minimum Data Set for Each Movement

  • Case note identifier (barcode scan or manual entry).
  • From-location code and custodian.
  • To-location code and custodian.
  • Date and time of dispatch and receipt (if different).
  • Reason for movement.
  • Transport method (internal porter, secure bag, external courier).

6. Functional Requirements

6.1 Registration of Case Notes

  • Each new case note file must be assigned a unique local case note number and barcode.
  • Registration must record patient identifiers and initial storage location.
  • Any new volume created must be linked to the same patient and tracked as a separate physical item.

6.2 Requesting Case Notes

  • Clinics and wards must be able to request case notes via a standard request form or electronic request.
  • Requests must include patient identifiers, clinic/ward, date/time required and priority (routine/urgent).
  • The system must record the request and link it to subsequent movements.

6.3 Issuing and Dispatch

  • Medical Records staff must record each case note leaving the library, including destination and custodian.
  • Barcodes should be scanned where available; otherwise, identifiers must be checked and recorded manually.
  • Case notes must be transported in secure, labelled containers with destination and return instructions.

6.4 Receipt and Local Tracking

  • Receiving departments must confirm receipt (scan or sign) and update the central register or local log.
  • Within a department, sub-movements (e.g. from ward station to consultant office) must be recorded in a local log if the case note leaves the main storage area for more than a defined threshold (e.g. 4 hours).

6.5 Return and Re-filing

  • After use, case notes must be returned to Medical Records or onward destination as specified.
  • Medical Records must record return, check completeness, and re-file in the correct sequence.
  • Any missing documents or inserts must be logged and investigated.

6.6 Temporary Records

  • Where the main case note is unavailable, a temporary record may be created.
  • Temporary records must be clearly labelled as such and tracked as separate items.
  • Once the main case note is located, temporary records must be merged or closed according to local policy, with tracking updated.

6.7 Off-site Storage and Retrieval

  • Case notes sent to off-site storage must be tracked with container IDs and storage location codes.
  • Requests for retrieval from off-site storage must be logged and linked to movements.
  • Service-level times for retrieval must be defined and monitored.

7. Non-functional Requirements

7.1 Information Governance and Compliance

  • All processes must comply with the NHS Wales Records Management Code of Practice and relevant data protection legislation.
  • Access to tracking information must be restricted to authorised staff.
  • Audit trails must be retained in line with retention schedules for health records and corporate records.

7.2 Security

  • Case notes must be stored in secure areas with controlled access (e.g. swipe card, key-controlled rooms).
  • Transport must use sealed or lockable containers where appropriate.
  • Incidents involving lost or misplaced case notes must be reported via local incident reporting systems and investigated.

7.3 Performance and Service Levels

  • Targets for case note availability (e.g. 95% of notes available in clinic 24 hours before appointment) must be defined.
  • Turnaround times for urgent requests must be specified (e.g. within 2 hours on-site).
  • Key performance indicators (KPIs) must be monitored and reported to governance groups.

8. Integration with Digital Systems

While this specification focuses on paper case notes, it must integrate conceptually and operationally with digital record initiatives such as the Welsh Nursing Care Record and emerging electronic health records.

  • Tracking identifiers (e.g. case note number, NHS number) must match those used in digital systems.
  • Clinic lists and admission systems should indicate whether a paper case note is expected or whether care is fully digital.
  • Where digital records replace paper for specific services, tracking rules for residual paper documents must be updated.

9. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Medical Records Manager: overall responsibility for the tracking system, policies and performance.
  • Medical Records Staff: registration, issuing, receiving, filing and maintenance of the central register.
  • Clinical Departments: local custody, logging of sub-movements, timely return of case notes.
  • Portering/Courier Services: secure transport and adherence to movement recording procedures.
  • Information Governance Lead: oversight of compliance, incident management and audit.

10. Governance and Audit

  • Regular audits of tracking accuracy (e.g. spot checks comparing register location vs. physical location).
  • Review of lost/misplaced case note incidents and corrective actions.
  • Annual review of policies to ensure alignment with national codes and local organisational changes.

11. Implementation Guidance

  • Develop local standard operating procedures (SOPs) based on this specification.
  • Train all relevant staff on tracking processes, security and incident reporting.
  • Introduce barcoding and electronic tracking where feasible, with paper-based fallback procedures.
  • Align implementation with wider digital programmes to avoid duplication and ensure a clear roadmap away from paper where appropriate.
